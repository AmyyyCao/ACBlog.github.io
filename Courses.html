courses

First year can be tough, but so are you!

APS100

orientation to engineering

The easiest course you'll be taking all year, make sure to submit all assignments on time. 

APS105

computer fundamentals

This course covers programming in C, start early on labs to ensure you have enough time. Stack Overflow is a great resource to ask for help. 

APS111

engineering strategies & practices i

Collaborating well with your team members will give you a much more enjoyable time. This course will require a lot of planning, try to ask TA for feedback in advance. 

APS112

engineering strategies & practices ii

Very similar to APS111, but clients will be unique to groups. Plan accordingly for other courses, as meetings are mandatory. 

MAT186

calculus i

Lots and lots of integration, practice questions in the textbook are great for studying. 

MAT187

calculus ii

Calculators aren't allowed, so material is a lot more theoretical. Lectures are super helpful, but keep up with the practice questions. 

mat188

linear algebra

You'll also learn a bit of MATLAB in this course. Think of the vectors unit from grade 12 calculus, but much more advanced. 

che112

physical chemistry

Combination of physics and chemistry, textbook isn't too helpful but problem sets are. 

che113

concepts in chemical engineering

Lots of circuit analysis as well as chemistry, exam questions are very similar to problem sets. 

mse101

introduction to materials science

Google will be your best friend. The textbook is written by the professor and very comprehensive. Spend time making notes and doing practice problems. 

civ100

mechanics

Think of dynamics from highschool physics, but much more advanced. Concepts can be difficult to grasp initially, so try using Youtube for explanations.
